Unit 2

AI vs Traditional Software

Estimated Learning Time: 10 minutes

Understanding the Fundamental Difference

Healthcare has relied on software for decades‚Äîelectronic health records, appointment systems, billing platforms, and laboratory systems. These systems are familiar, predictable, and rule-driven.

Artificial Intelligence is different.

It does not follow a fixed set of instructions. Instead, it identifies patterns in data and estimates the likelihood of future outcomes.

Understanding this distinction is critical for safe and effective clinical use.

Core Distinction (Key Message)

Traditional software follows rules.

AI estimates probabilities.

Traditional software executes instructions written by humans

AI systems learn from historical data and apply that learning to new situations

Traditional Rule-Based Alert

System response: "Potential interaction ‚Äì increased bleeding risk"

Strength: Reliable and conservative

Key Clinical Takeaway

Traditional software tells you what to do.

AI helps you decide what to pay attention to.

They are complementary‚Äînot competing.

When Traditional Software Is Preferable

Use rule-based systems when you need:

  • Clear dosing calculations
  • Safety-critical alerts
  • Regulatory and compliance checks
  • Tasks requiring 100% consistency
Example:
  • Insulin dose calculators
  • Drug allergy alerts

When AI Adds Value

AI is useful when:

  • Patterns are complex
  • Multiple variables interact
  • Trends matter more than single values
  • Text, images, or signals must be analysed
Examples in healthcare:
  • Imaging support (X-ray, ECG pattern recognition)
  • Clinical documentation summarisation
  • Risk stratification for follow-up care

Safety Perspective (Why This Matters)

Safety Perspective (Why This Matters)

AI outputs are not facts.

They are estimates based on prior data.

Therefore:

  • AI recommendations must always be reviewed by clinicians
  • AI systems require monitoring, validation, and governance
  • Responsibility for care remains with the healthcare professional

Final Message for Clinicians

AI does not replace clinical reasoning.

It changes how information is presented‚Äîfrom rules to risks.

Good clinical care = human judgment + appropriate tools.

Question 1

How is an AI-generated recommendation different from a traditional rule-based alert in healthcare software?

Answer:

A traditional rule-based alert gives a fixed response based on predefined rules (for example, triggering an alert when a value crosses a threshold). An AI-generated recommendation estimates the likelihood or risk of an outcome based on patterns learned from data. AI outputs are probabilistic and must be interpreted in clinical context, whereas rule-based alerts are deterministic and predictable.

Question 2

Why must clinicians always review and interpret AI outputs rather than act on them automatically?

Answer:

Because AI systems do not understand clinical meaning or patient intent‚Äîthey only detect patterns in data. Their outputs reflect probabilities, not certainties. Clinical judgment is essential to assess relevance, safety, and applicability to the individual patient, ensuring that AI supports care rather than replacing professional responsibility.

Artificial Intelligence is not confined to a single specialty or professional group. It is increasingly integrated across all areas of healthcare, including medicine, nursing, allied health, mental health, dentistry, pharmacy, public health, and healthcare administration. Understanding how AI is applied across these disciplines enables health professionals to identify both opportunities and potential risks within their own scope of practice, supporting safe, effective, and responsible use of AI in healthcare.
